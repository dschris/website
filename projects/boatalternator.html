Boat Alternator Conversion

190HP Mercruiser

Briefing

This is a Mercruiser 190HP on a 19-foot 1986 Thompson bowrider. The motor was originally designed for use with a regulator. Since an alternator is a more desirable setup for mainting a well-charged battery, we went ahead and ordered the swap kit. The swap kit comes with the alternator, wires, an arm that the alternator will mount to, and a new belt to account for the alternator.

Getting Started

Removing the regulator is not hard. Aside from some unfriendly angles in the compartment in the back of the Thompson, it's more or less a straight socket wrench job. I removed the regulator wire and then the regulator itself in about 15 minutes and then started prepping to get the alternator ready for installation. Unfortunately when I did this project I wasn't planning on documenting it for anything like some of my other projects so I only have after pictures to show. Here's a shot of the alternator from the top showing the positioning in the engine compartment and a shot from the front showing the arm that it mounts to.



Conclusion

Pictured left you can take a look at the alternator, installed, from the rear. The red wire there connects directly to the battery. Ultimately this isn't a hard swap and takes little autmotive or electrical knowledge. If you're running an old Mercuriser with a regulator, I do recommend getting the kit and going ahead with the job. You'll get that clumsy regulator out of there and set yourself up with something a bit more reliable. The project shouldn't take you more than 2 hours and you can be back on the water in no time.
